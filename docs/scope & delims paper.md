Scope and Delimitations
Scope
This project focuses on the design and development of iReside, an integrated property and tenant management ecosystem optimized for the multi-unit rental market within Barangay Marulas, Valenzuela City. The system comprises three primary interfaces: a Discovery Portal for prospective tenants, a Visual Command Center for landlords and property managers, and an Administrative Oversight Portal for system moderation. The platform is delivered through a unified web-based architecture built with Next.js 16 and React 19, featuring both a desktop-optimized dashboard for comprehensive management tasks and a mobile-optimized Progressive Web App (PWA) interface for on-the-go operations. This responsive, cross-platform approach ensures that users can access the system across diverse devices through any modern web browser, with mobile-specific UI patterns and touch-optimized interactions while maintaining feature parity across form factors. System security is enforced through a multi-layered approach using Supabase Auth and PostgreSQL Row Level Security (RLS), maintaining strict data isolation between three defined roles: Super Admin, Landlord, and Tenant.
The core of the system is a 2D Spatial Canvas featuring a Modular Floor Planner, where landlords can digitally map building layouts using a drag-and-drop structural engine with grid-snapping for precise unit alignment. This is enhanced by Dynamic Status Overlays that provide real-time visual alerts for unit occupancy, overdue payments, and active maintenance requests. The system manages individual unit listings via a multi-step wizard, allowing for granular pricing, deposit rules, and amenity tagging. The 2D canvas is strictly an organizational visualization tool and does not produce engineering-grade measurements or structural assessments.
The tenant-facing experience begins with a Map-based Discovery Portal using Leaflet.js to provide location-aware radius filters. Tenants can track their journey through a Live Application Tracking interface that provides real-time status updates and a visual application timeline. The system includes a secure Digital Lease Signing workflow with canvas-based electronic signatures and a Renewal Eligibility Engine that calculates upcoming renewal windows based on historical lease records. The canvas-based signature serves as proof of agreement intent; parties requiring legal notarization must complete that process separately with a commissioned notary public.
iReside implements a comprehensive Real-Time Financial Ledger that synchronizes overdue, pending, and completed rent and utility invoices. The system supports Dynamic Invoice Breakdowns covering base rent, water, and electricity charges, as well as a localized Manual Payment Verification system where tenants upload GCash receipts for landlord approval. Landlords can also generate professional PDF reports and CSV data exports for auditing and historical performance snapshots. The financial ledger is designed for operational tracking only and is not a substitute for formal accounting systems; it does not execute actual fund transfers, integrate with payment gateways or banking APIs, or compute tax obligations and government remittances.
The system integrates supportive AI utilities powered by Groq Llama 3.1 via Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). These include the iRis AI Assistant, which provides tenants with contextual intelligence regarding building rules and local property amenities; AI-Driven Landlord Analytics, which analyzes operational metrics such as net income, occupancy, and maintenance activity to surface strategic recommendations; Real-Time Message Moderation, which filters toxic content, hate speech, and spam within communication channels; and Maintenance Triage, which uses natural language processing to categorize the severity of maintenance reports and provide initial cost estimates. All AI outputs are strictly advisory and require human confirmation before any action is taken. The AI does not autonomously approve budgets, contracts, or legal decisions, and does not perform credit scoring or automated tenant background checks.
A dedicated Admin Portal provides system-wide monitoring through Live Enterprise Metrics covering total users, active leases, and the registration pipeline. Administrators can perform Advanced Applicant Review for landlord registrations, vetting uploaded business permits and government IDs. The portal includes tools for user governance and moderation of public listings. Real-time communication is facilitated by a Real-Time Messaging Engine with presence indicators and media sharing capabilities, limited to in-app text exchanges. Communication records are retained for operational reference, though they do not meet enterprise-grade archiving standards and are not designed for legal discovery purposes.
Delimitations
The study is subject to the following constraints and exclusions, each of which reflects deliberate boundaries set to maintain research focus, operational feasibility, and legal compliance.
A. Geographic and Demographic Boundaries
The system is limited to residential multi-unit properties — apartments, dormitories, and boarding houses — within Barangay Marulas, Valenzuela City. Single-family homes, transient hotels, and commercial properties are excluded, as these require distinct regulatory frameworks and management workflows not applicable to multi-unit residential tenancy. Expansion beyond Marulas is not attempted, as doing so would require coordination with multiple barangay councils, dilute the research focus, and prevent the team from establishing the local trust and physical verification capacity necessary for meaningful user testing. The discovery map relies on landlord-provided coordinates rather than cadastral parcel data, as the latter is not publicly accessible and requires legal interpretation beyond the team's qualifications. Tenant demographics are not segmented by age, income, or employment type, in observance of Data Privacy Act requirements and to avoid enabling discriminatory filtering within the platform.
B. Property and Unit Modeling Constraints
The 2D Spatial Canvas is an organizational visualization tool only and does not produce engineering-grade measurements, structural load computations, building code compliance assessments, or fire safety evaluations. These activities constitute professional practice restricted to licensed engineers and architects under Philippine law. The floor planner uses a simplified rectangular grid and does not support irregular or curved geometry, as such features would introduce significant development complexity while offering minimal added value given that the vast majority of multi-unit residential properties in Marulas use standard rectangular unit layouts. Three-dimensional rendering, photorealistic visualization, and BIM integration are likewise excluded. Unit status overlays are informational and do not automatically trigger maintenance dispatch or penalty enforcement, as automated actions of this nature require verified contractor networks and a liability framework the platform has not established. The system does not generate automated eviction notices, as eviction is a judicial process requiring attorney supervision under Batas Pambansa Blg. 25.
C. Discovery and Search Constraints
The discovery portal provides radius-based filtering only. Advanced spatial analytics such as walkability scores, crime overlays, and flood hazard maps are excluded, as the underlying datasets are either classified, unavailable via public API, or maintained only in non-standardized formats by government agencies. The map does not provide turn-by-turn navigation, real-time traffic data, or commute-time optimization, as these features require third-party routing APIs with associated costs and liability exposure. Public listing moderation is handled through admin approval and does not include syndication to external marketplaces, which would require commercial agreements and ongoing data synchronization beyond the scope of this research.
D. Messaging and Communication Limits
Messaging is limited to in-app text and does not include voice calls or video conferencing, which would require WebRTC infrastructure and significant bandwidth resources inconsistent with the target demographic's connectivity. The system uses standard HTTPS/TLS transport security and database encryption at rest but does not implement end-to-end encryption, as doing so would prevent admin moderation and create key recovery complications for legally significant communications such as lease discussions and payment confirmations. The platform does not integrate with external SMS gateways or email marketing systems, nor does it support user-programmable notification workflows. Automated notification rules are limited to predefined events such as payment reminders, maintenance updates, and lease expiration alerts, which cover the majority of operational communication needs.
E. Lease and Document Handling
The digital lease signing workflow supports canvas-based signatures but does not provide third-party notarization, as remote digital notarization is not legally recognized in the Philippines and requires physical appearance before a commissioned notary public under the 2004 Rules on Notarial Practice. The platform does not assess the legal enforceability of uploaded contracts, provide dynamically generated legal clause templates, manage dispute resolution or court filing processes, or perform automated redlining and version comparison of lease amendments. These activities either constitute the practice of law under Rule 138 of the Rules of Court or require legal expertise and ongoing monitoring of legislative changes that are beyond the scope of this study. Document templates are provided in basic form with clearly marked customization sections, with the expectation that landlords consult legal counsel for contract-specific provisions.
F. Financial Operations and Payments
The system records payment schedules and invoice history but does not execute actual fund transfers or integrate with payment gateways, bank APIs, or GCash APIs. Operating as a payment intermediary would require BSP registration under Circular 649, AMLA compliance, KYC infrastructure, and PCI-DSS certification — obligations that are not feasible within the scope of an academic research project. Receipt verification is performed manually by landlords reviewing uploaded GCash screenshots, and no automated OCR-based fraud detection is included, as this would require training data and API access that are not publicly available. The ledger does not compute tax obligations, VAT filings, or government remittance schedules, and is not a substitute for formal accounting books maintained under BIR regulations. Currency support is limited to Philippine Peso.
G. Maintenance and Work Orders
Maintenance requests are categorized through AI assistance but do not automatically assign contractors, as doing so would require a vetted vendor network and a formal liability framework. The platform does not include vendor bidding, procurement workflows, inventory management, or integration with external facilities management platforms. Work order scheduling is basic and does not optimize staff routing or labor allocation. Building inspection schedules, safety certification renewals, and compliance tracking remain the landlord's responsibility and are not managed within the system.
H. AI Utility Boundaries
All AI-generated outputs within iReside are advisory and require human confirmation before any action is taken. The AI does not autonomously approve budgets, contracts, or legal decisions, and does not provide legal advice or guarantee the accuracy of its recommendations, as doing so would constitute unauthorized practice of law without the licensure required under Rule 138 of the Rules of Court. The AI knowledge base is configured by landlords from curated content and does not perform real-time web scraping, which would introduce unreliable sources, copyright complications, and unpredictable outputs. Credit scoring and automated tenant background checks are excluded due to the unavailability of credit bureau partnerships, the Data Privacy Act's requirements for human review of automated decisions affecting individuals, and the risk of algorithmic bias against informal workers and unbanked populations.
I. Administrative Governance
Admin vetting validates document presence and performs visual inspection of uploaded permits and IDs but does not authenticate documents against government databases. The Valenzuela City Business Permit and Licensing Office (BPLO) and other relevant agencies do not provide public-facing APIs, and formal data-sharing agreements require institutional accreditation not available to this research project. Admin tools do not include full compliance auditing, periodic regulatory reporting, or management of landlord licensing renewals and tax registration. The admin role is limited to platform policy enforcement and does not extend to arbitrating legal disputes, which must be resolved through barangay conciliation or the appropriate courts under existing Philippine law.
J. Security and Privacy Constraints
The system employs role-based access control with strong authentication but does not implement zero-trust networking, military-grade encryption, or enterprise SIEM integrations, as these exceed the security requirements and budget of a research-phase rental management platform. Data is hosted on cloud infrastructure; on-premise deployments are not supported. The platform requires an active internet connection and does not support offline-first synchronization, as conflict resolution for offline data modifications would introduce significant architectural complexity. Biometric authentication is excluded due to Data Privacy Act obligations for sensitive personal information, liveness detection requirements, and device compatibility constraints. Government-issued IDs submitted during landlord vetting are retained only as long as necessary for verification purposes, consistent with data minimization principles under RA 10173.
K. Device, Platform, and Integration Limits
The platform is delivered as a unified web application built with Next.js 16 and React 19, providing both desktop-optimized and mobile-optimized Progressive Web App (PWA) interfaces through responsive design patterns. The mobile experience features touch-optimized UI components, screen-based navigation, and mobile-first CSS modules that adapt to smaller viewports while maintaining full feature access. The system supports all modern web browsers on desktop, Android, and iOS devices through standard web technologies. Native mobile applications for Android or iOS are not included in the current version, as developing and maintaining separate native codebases would exceed the capstone timeline and budget, and the PWA approach provides adequate cross-platform coverage for the target user base. The system does not provide open APIs for third-party developers, integrate with government housing or national ID verification databases, or connect with external listing marketplaces. Data export is limited to operational PDF reports and CSV files; large-scale data warehousing, ETL pipelines, and BI platform integrations are outside the scope of this study. Push notifications may be delivered through browser-based web push APIs where supported by the user's device and browser.
L. Analytics, Reporting, and Content
Reporting within the system is operational and descriptive. Predictive analytics, forecasting, and dynamic pricing optimization are excluded, as these require multi-year historical datasets, external economic data integrations, and machine learning expertise beyond the research team's capacity. The system does not include a custom BI dashboard builder or blend external data sources into its analytics. Listing photo authenticity is verified only through basic format validation and duplicate detection; reverse image search and computer vision fraud detection are not implemented. The platform does not support 360-degree virtual tours, AR features, or 3D rendering. Media is compressed and stored for web-optimized performance rather than archival quality. The system does not provide multilingual localization beyond English with basic Filipino terminology, as the target audience of Marulas residents is served adequately by this combination.
M. Legal, Ethical, and Operational Boundaries
The platform does not provide legal counsel on tenancy disputes or eviction procedures, generate government compliance reports, or make automated housing denial decisions. Any automated decision-making that could affect tenant rights must involve meaningful human review in compliance with Section 20 of the Data Privacy Act (RA 10173). The system does not collect biometric, medical, or financial credit data, consistent with data minimization principles and fair housing considerations. Terms of use and privacy policies included in this research version are generic and should be reviewed by legal counsel prior to any commercial deployment. The platform assumes that landlord-provided data is accurate and does not perform independent third-party verification of property details. Standard cloud hosting availability applies; the system does not guarantee high availability SLAs, and disaster recovery is limited to regular database backups. The current version does not support automated lease renewal or IoT integrations, and does not include advanced GIS layers, cadastral mapping, multi-city scaling, or an iOS version — all of which are identified as future work contingent on regulatory partnership, infrastructure investment, and post-research commercialization.
