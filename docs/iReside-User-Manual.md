## 1. Welcome to iReside

### 1.1 What is iReside?

iReside is a web-based platform that helps landlords manage their properties and tenants. Whether you are a landlord overseeing multiple buildings, a tenant paying rent and submitting maintenance requests, or an administrator keeping everything running smoothly — iReside brings everything together in one place.

The platform handles the full cycle of property management:

- Finding and approving new tenants
- Creating and signing lease agreements
- Collecting rent and tracking payments
- Responding to maintenance issues
- Communicating between landlords and tenants
- Building a sense of community within a property

### 1.2 Who Uses iReside?

| Role | What They Do |
|---|---|
| **Landlord** | Property owner or building manager who oversees units, tenants, and operations |
| **Tenant** | Someone renting a unit within a property |
| **Admin** | System administrator who manages user accounts and oversees the platform |

Each role has its own workspace — you only see what is relevant to you.

### 1.3 Supported Devices

iReside works on any modern device with a web browser:

- Desktop and laptop computers (Windows, Mac, Linux)
- Tablets (iPad, Android tablets)
- Mobile phones (iPhone, Android)

For the best experience, use the latest version of Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, or Apple Safari.

You can also install iReside on your phone or desktop so it works like a regular app (see section 17.5).

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 Accessing iReside

iReside is invite-only. You will receive an email invitation before you can use the platform.

1. Open your web browser
2. Go to the web address provided by your landlord or administrator
3. You will see the iReside welcome page

### 2.2 Logging In

1. Click the **Login** button on the welcome page
2. Enter your **email address** and **password** (these were sent to you in your invitation email)
3. Click **Sign In**

You will be taken to your personal dashboard.

### 2.3 First Time Logging In

If this is your first time:

- You may be asked to set a new password
- A short interactive tour may guide you through the main features
- You can take this tour again later from your Settings page

### 2.4 Forgotten Password

1. On the login screen, click **Forgot Password**
2. Enter your email address
3. Check your inbox for a password reset email
4. Click the link in the email and enter a new password
5. Log in with your new password

### 2.5 Logging Out

Click your profile icon or name in the sidebar, then click **Log Out**.

## 3. Understanding Your Dashboard

### 3.1 Screen Layout

Every page in iReside follows the same basic layout:

- **Sidebar** (left side) — the main menu. Click any item to go to that section.
- **Main Area** (center) — where the active page is displayed.
- **Top Bar** — shows your current section along with controls like dark mode toggle.

On smaller screens (phones, tablets), the sidebar collapses into a button with three horizontal lines (sometimes called a "hamburger menu"). Tap it to open the menu.

### 3.2 Dark Mode

Look for the sun or moon icon in the sidebar or top bar:

- Click the **sun icon** to switch to dark mode
- Click the **moon icon** to switch back to light mode

Dark mode uses darker background colors, which can be easier on the eyes in low-light environments.

### 3.3 Notifications

A bell icon or banner at the top of the page may alert you to:

- New messages
- Maintenance updates
- Payment confirmations
- Lease signing requests

Click the bell to view all recent notifications.

## 4. For Landlords

This section describes every page and feature available to landlords.

### 4.1 Landlord Dashboard

When you log in, you will see your dashboard — a summary of everything happening across your properties:

- **Total properties** you manage
- **Total units** across all properties
- **Active leases** currently in effect
- **Pending maintenance** requests needing your attention
- **Revenue chart** showing rental income over time
- **Recent activity** — a list of the latest events (new leases signed, payments received, etc.)

### 4.2 Properties

This page shows all the buildings or properties you manage.

- Each property is displayed as a card with its name, address, number of units, and occupancy
- Click a property to see its details — units, tenants, and activity

**To add a new property:**

1. Click **Add Property**
2. Fill in the name and address
3. Add a description (optional)
4. Select the amenities available (pool, gym, parking, etc.)
5. Upload photos (optional)
6. Click **Save**

### 4.3 Tenants

A list of all tenants across your properties.

- Each row shows: name, email, property, unit, and lease status
- Use the search bar to find a specific tenant
- Click a tenant to view their full profile, lease details, payment history, and maintenance requests

### 4.4 Applications

When a prospective tenant submits an application, it appears here:

- **Pending** — new applications waiting for your review
- **Approved** — applications you have accepted
- **Rejected** — applications you have declined

Click an application to see the applicant's details, employment information, preferred unit, and submitted documents.

To approve an applicant, open the application, review the information, and click **Approve**. You can then proceed to create a lease.

### 4.5 Leases

This page lists all lease agreements.

- See the status of each lease (Draft, Active, Expiring, Expired)
- Click a lease to view or download the signed PDF
- See which leases need your signature

### 4.6 Maintenance

All maintenance requests submitted by your tenants appear here, sorted by urgency.

- **Urgent** issues (flooding, gas leaks, no power) are shown first with a red highlight
- **Standard** issues appear below

Click any request to:

- See the full description and photos from the tenant
- Add notes or instructions
- Mark the request as **In Progress** when you start working on it
- Mark it as **Resolved** when the issue is fixed

The tenant is notified each time you update the status.

### 4.7 Messages

Your inbox for communicating with tenants.

- Conversations are listed with the most recent message first
- Unread conversations are highlighted
- A number shows how many unread messages you have

Click a conversation to open and reply. See Section 11 for details.

### 4.8 Documents

A central place for important files:

- Signed lease agreements
- Official receipts
- Property photos and documents
- Move-out inspection reports

You can view, download, upload, or delete documents here.

### 4.9 Invoices

See all invoices generated for your tenants:

- Filter by tenant, property, or date
- See which invoices are paid and which are overdue
- Mark invoices as paid (for cash or bank transfer payments)
- Download invoices as PDF files

### 4.10 Analytics

Charts and graphs showing trends across your portfolio:

- **Occupancy rate** — how many of your units are rented over time
- **Revenue trends** — monthly income from rent
- **Maintenance patterns** — what kinds of issues come up most often
- **Tenant turnover** — how often tenants move out

### 4.11 Unit Map

A visual layout of your property showing all units.

- Each unit is color-coded: **Green** (vacant), **Blue** (occupied), **Amber** (payment due), **Red** (under maintenance)
- Click any unit to see details
- Switch between different floors
- Toggle to a 3D view for a more immersive look

### 4.12 Utilities

Record and manage utility meter readings for your units:

- Enter current meter readings for electricity, water, or gas
- The system calculates consumption since the last reading
- Charges are added to the tenant's next invoice

### 4.13 Utility Billing

A separate view focused on utility charges:

- See which tenants have outstanding utility balances
- Generate utility bills
- Track payment status for utility charges

### 4.14 Community

Manage social features for your property:

- Make announcements to all tenants
- Create posts visible to the community
- Moderate comments and shared photos
- Set community rules that tenants can view

### 4.15 Move-Out

When a tenant is leaving, use this section to manage the process:

- Initiate a move-out request
- Schedule a unit inspection
- Document the unit condition with photos
- Process the security deposit return (with itemized deductions if needed)

### 4.16 Onboarding

If you are a new landlord, this step-by-step guide helps you get started:

- Set up your profile
- Add your first property
- Learn about key features

### 4.17 Settings

- Update your name, email, or phone number
- Change your password
- Choose what notifications you receive

## 5. For Tenants

This section describes every page and feature available to tenants.

### 5.1 Tenant Dashboard

When you log in, you will see your personal overview:

- **Lease status** — when your lease started and when it ends
- **Rent status** — how much is due and when payment is expected
- **Open maintenance requests** — any issues you have reported that are still being worked on
- **Recent activity** — notifications about payments, messages from your landlord, and community announcements

### 5.2 Lease

View the details of your lease agreement:

- Monthly rent amount
- Security deposit amount
- Lease start and end dates
- Your property and unit details

You can also download a PDF copy of your signed lease agreement.

### 5.3 Signing a Lease

If your landlord has sent you a lease to sign:

1. You will receive an email notification
2. Log in and go to **Lease**
3. Click **Sign Lease**
4. Review the terms carefully
5. Draw your signature using your mouse, finger (on touchscreen), or stylus on the signature pad
6. Click **Confirm**

Once you sign, your landlord may also need to sign. After both parties have signed, the lease becomes active.

### 5.4 Payments

**Viewing your payment history:**

- Go to **Payments** to see your complete payment ledger
- Each entry shows: date, amount, what it was for (rent, utilities, etc.), and payment status
- Download official receipts for any completed payment

**Making a payment:**

1. Go to **Payments**
2. See your outstanding balance
3. Click the invoice you want to pay
4. Follow the checkout instructions
5. After payment, you will receive a confirmation and an official receipt

### 5.5 Maintenance

**To report an issue:**

1. Go to **Maintenance**
2. Click **New Request**
3. Select a category:
   - **Plumbing** — leaking pipes, clogged drains, toilet problems
   - **Electrical** — power outages, faulty switches
   - **Appliance** — air conditioner, refrigerator, water heater
   - **Structural** — cracks, doors or windows not working
   - **Pest Control**
   - **General** — anything else
4. Write a brief **title** (for example: "Kitchen sink leaking")
5. Write a **description** with more details
6. Optionally upload **photos** of the issue
7. Check **Self-Repair** if you plan to fix it yourself
8. Click **Submit**

**Tracking your request:**

- Go to **Maintenance** to see all your requests
- Each request shows its current status (Pending, In Progress, Resolved, Closed)
- You will be notified when your landlord responds

### 5.6 Messages

Your inbox for communicating with your landlord.

- View all your conversations
- Unread messages are marked clearly
- Send messages with text, emoji, or attached photos
- See when your landlord has read your message

### 5.7 Community

Connect with your building or community:

- View announcements from your landlord
- See community posts and photos
- Like and comment on posts
- Read the community rules

### 5.8 Unit Map

Explore your building's layout:

- See where your unit is located
- View common areas and amenities
- Toggle between 2D floorplan and 3D view

### 5.9 Utilities

See your utility usage and charges:

- Current and past meter readings for your unit
- Utility charges itemized on your invoices
- Track your consumption over time

### 5.10 Profile

View and update your personal information:

- Full name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Emergency contact
- Profile photo (you can crop it after uploading)

### 5.11 Settings

- Change your password
- Choose what notifications you receive

### 5.12 Product Tour

If you would like a guided walkthrough of the tenant portal again, go to **Tour** from the sidebar.

### 5.13 iRis Assistant

A helpful assistant that can answer your questions about the property, your lease, and building amenities. See Section 13 for full details.

## 7. Managing Properties

2. Click **Add Property**
3. Fill in the following:
   - **Property Name** — for example, "Sunrise Residences"

   - **Monthly Rent** — the rental amount in Philippine Pesos

4. Click **Save**

### 7.3 Unit Status Colors

Each unit is shown with a color that tells you its status at a glance:

| Color | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Green** | Unit is vacant and available for rent |
| **Blue** | Unit is currently occupied |

### 7.4 Editing a Property or Unit

1. Open the property
2. Click the **Edit** button next to the property or unit
3. Make your changes
4. Click **Save**

## 8. Leases & Signing

### 8.1 What is a Lease?

A lease is a legal agreement between a landlord and a tenant for renting a unit. It defines:

- Who is renting which unit
- How much the monthly rent is
- How long the lease lasts (start and end dates)
- The security deposit amount
- Any special terms or conditions

### 8.2 Creating a Lease (Landlords)

1. Go to **Tenants** and select the tenant
2. Click **Create Lease**
3. Enter the lease details:
   - **Start Date** — when the lease begins
   - **End Date** — when the lease ends
   - **Monthly Rent** — the agreed amount
   - **Security Deposit** — the deposit amount
   - **Special Terms** — any additional clauses (optional)
4. Click **Create**

### 8.3 Signing a Lease (Tenants)

1. You will receive an email when a lease is ready for your signature
2. Log in to your account
3. Go to **Lease** or **Sign Lease**
4. Read through the terms carefully
5. Use the **signature pad** to draw your signature with your mouse, finger, or stylus
6. Click **Confirm Signing**
7. You will receive a confirmation email once the signing is complete

### 8.4 Lease Statuses

| Status | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Being prepared, not yet signed |
| **Active** | Signed by all parties and in effect |
| **Expiring** | Will end within 30 days |
| **Expired** | Lease term has ended |
| **Closed** | Finalized, all obligations settled |

### 8.5 Downloading Lease Documents

Both landlords and tenants can download lease agreements as PDF files:

1. Go to **Leases** (landlord) or **Lease** (tenant)
2. Click the lease you want to view
3. Click **Download PDF**

## 9. Payments & Billing

### 9.1 Overview

Landlords generate invoices for monthly rent and any additional charges. Tenants can see what they owe and make payments. All amounts are in **Philippine Pesos (₱)** .

### 9.2 Viewing Invoices (Tenants)

1. Go to **Payments**
2. You will see a list of your invoices
3. Each invoice shows: date, description (rent, utilities), amount, and payment status
4. Paid invoices have an **Official Receipt** you can download

### 9.3 Making a Payment (Tenants)

1. Go to **Payments**
2. Find the unpaid invoice you want to settle
3. Click **Pay Now** or the invoice itself
4. Follow the on-screen instructions to complete payment
5. After successful payment, you will receive a confirmation

### 9.4 Official Receipts

After a payment is recorded, an official receipt is generated. It contains:

- Receipt number
- Your name and unit
- Payment date
- Amount paid
- Breakdown of charges (rent, utilities, deposit)
- Landlord or company details

You can download receipts as PDF files from the Payments page.

### 9.5 Tracking Payments (Landlords)

1. Go to **Invoices**
2. See all invoices across your properties
3. Filter by tenant, property, or date
4. See which invoices are paid and which are overdue
5. Mark invoices as paid if you receive cash or bank transfer payments outside the system

### 9.6 Security Deposits

- Security deposits are tracked per lease
- When a tenant moves out, you can process the deposit return
- If there are deductions (for damages), they are listed with explanations

## 10. Maintenance Requests

### 10.1 Submitting a Request (Tenants)

1. Go to **Maintenance** and click **New Request**
2. Choose a **category** that best describes your issue
3. Give your request a short **title** (for example: "Leaking faucet in bathroom")
4. Write a **description** with more details about the problem
5. Optionally attach **photos** so the landlord can see the issue
6. If you intend to fix it yourself, check **Self-Repair**
7. Click **Submit**

### 10.2 Urgency

The system automatically sorts requests by urgency. Certain keywords (like "fire", "flood", "gas leak", "no power") flag your request as urgent so the landlord sees it right away.

### 10.3 Tracking Status

You can check the progress of your request at any time:

| Status | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | Awaiting landlord review |
| **In Progress** | Landlord is working on it |
| **Resolved** | Issue has been fixed. Please confirm if everything is okay |
| **Closed** | You have confirmed the issue is resolved |

### 10.4 Responding to Requests (Landlords)

1. Go to **Maintenance**
2. Check urgent items first (they appear at the top)
3. Click a request to view full details and photos
4. You can: acknowledge, add notes, mark as In Progress, or mark as Resolved

The tenant is notified each time you update the status.

## 17. Troubleshooting

### 17.1 Login Problems

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| Forgot password | Click "Forgot Password" on the login screen and follow the email instructions |
| Account locked | Contact your landlord or system administrator |
| Can't access a page | Make sure you are logged in with the correct account type |
| Logged out suddenly | Your session may have expired. Just log in again |

### 17.2 Payment Issues

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| Payment not showing up | Wait a few minutes and refresh. Contact your landlord if it persists |
| Wrong amount | Check the invoice. Contact your landlord if there is an error |
| Need a receipt copy | Go to **Payments**, find the completed payment, and click **Download Receipt** |

### 17.3 Page Display Issues

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| Page not loading | Refresh the page. Clear your browser cache if it persists |
| Buttons not working | Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Edge) |
| Layout looks broken | Reset zoom (Ctrl+0 on Windows, Cmd+0 on Mac). Clear your cache |

### 17.4 Maintenance Issues

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| Emergency (fire, gas leak, flooding) | Call emergency services FIRST, then submit a maintenance request |
| Haven't heard back | Check your messages. Send a follow-up message to your landlord |
| Wrong category chosen | Contact your landlord directly to correct it |

### 17.5 Installing iReside on Your Device

**Android (Chrome):**
1. Open iReside in Chrome
2. Tap the three-dot menu
3. Tap **Add to Home screen**
4. Follow the instructions

**iPhone/iPad (Safari):**
1. Open iReside in Safari
2. Tap the **Share** button
3. Scroll down and tap **Add to Home Screen**

**Computer (Chrome or Edge):**
1. Open iReside
2. Click the install icon in the address bar or browser menu
3. Click **Install**
