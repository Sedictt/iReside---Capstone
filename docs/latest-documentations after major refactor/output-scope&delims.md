# 1.4 Scope and Delimitation

## Scope

This project focuses on the design and development of iReside, an exclusive, private property and tenant management ecosystem optimized for the multi-unit rental market within Valenzuela City. iReside operates as a landlord-centric management platform where each property functions as an isolated, private ecosystem — analogous to a private server — and tenants are provisioned exclusively through approved onboarding workflows. There is no public listing, unit searching, or marketplace discovery functionality.

The system comprises three primary interfaces: a **Landlord Portal** for comprehensive property and tenant management, a **Tenant Portal** for residency tools and communication, and a **Super Administrator Portal** for platform governance and system monitoring. The platform is delivered through a unified web-based architecture built with Next.js 16 and React 19, featuring both a desktop-optimized dashboard for comprehensive management tasks and a mobile-optimized Progressive Web App (PWA) interface for on-the-go operations. This responsive, cross-platform approach ensures that users can access the system across diverse devices through any modern web browser, with mobile-specific UI patterns and touch-optimized interactions while maintaining feature parity across form factors. System security is enforced through a multi-layered approach using Supabase Auth and PostgreSQL Row Level Security (RLS), maintaining strict data isolation between three defined roles: Super Admin, Landlord, and Tenant.

### 2D Spatial Canvas & Floor Planner

The core of the system is a 2D Spatial Canvas featuring a Modular Floor Planner, where landlords can digitally map building layouts using a drag-and-drop structural engine with grid-snapping for precise unit alignment. This is enhanced by Dynamic Status Overlays that provide real-time visual alerts for unit occupancy, overdue payments, and active maintenance requests. Tenants can view this floor plan as a read-only unit map and submit unit transfer requests directly from the visual interface for vacant units. The system manages individual unit configurations via a multi-step wizard, allowing for granular pricing, deposit rules, and amenity tagging. The 2D canvas is strictly an organizational visualization tool and does not produce engineering-grade measurements or structural assessments.

### Walk-in Applications & Tenant Onboarding

Since the system operates as a private, invitation-only platform, tenant onboarding is exclusively managed by landlords through a walk-in application workflow. Landlords open a unit showcase, record applicant details and supporting documents, and manage the application through a status pipeline (Pending → Reviewing → Approved). Upon approval, the system finalizes the application into a lease record and provisions a tenant account with credentials. There is no self-service tenant registration or public application portal — all tenant accounts originate from landlord-initiated or admin-initiated onboarding processes.

### Financial Management

iReside implements a comprehensive Real-Time Financial Ledger that synchronizes overdue, pending, and completed rent and utility invoices. The system supports Dynamic Invoice Breakdowns covering base rent, water, and electricity charges, and landlords can create and track invoices for their tenants. Landlords can also generate professional PDF reports and CSV data exports for auditing and historical performance snapshots. The financial ledger is designed for operational tracking only and is not a substitute for formal accounting systems; it does not execute actual fund transfers, integrate with payment gateways or banking APIs, or compute tax obligations and government remittances.

### AI-Powered Features

The system integrates supportive AI utilities powered by Groq Llama 3.1 8B via Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). These include the **iRis AI Assistant**, which provides tenants with contextual intelligence regarding building rules, property amenities, lease details, and recent payments; **AI-Driven Landlord Analytics**, which analyzes operational metrics such as net income, occupancy, and maintenance activity to surface strategic recommendations with a toggle between simplified and detailed modes; **Real-Time Message Moderation**, which filters toxic content, hate speech, and spam within communication channels; and **Maintenance Triage**, which uses natural language processing to categorize the severity of maintenance reports. All AI outputs are strictly advisory and require human confirmation before any action is taken. The AI does not autonomously approve budgets, contracts, or legal decisions, and does not perform credit scoring or automated tenant background checks.

### Communication & Community

A **Real-Time Messaging Engine** powered by Supabase Realtime facilitates instant communication between landlords and tenants, with presence indicators, typing indicators, read receipts, and media/file sharing capabilities within conversation threads. In addition, a **Community Hub** provides a centralized space for building-wide engagement, supporting discussion posts, photo albums, resident polls, management notices, utility alerts, threaded comments, post saving, and content reporting. Landlords manage community content through an approval queue and moderation dashboard. Communication is limited to in-app text exchanges and media sharing. Communication records are retained for operational reference, though they do not meet enterprise-grade archiving standards and are not designed for legal discovery purposes.

### Administrative Portal

A dedicated Admin Portal provides system-wide monitoring through Live Enterprise Metrics covering total users, active properties, active leases, and the registration pipeline. Administrators can perform Advanced Applicant Review for landlord registrations, vetting uploaded business permits and government IDs through detailed review modals. The portal includes tools for user governance, role-specific filtering, and internal administrative notes on registration cases.

### Tenant Experience & Onboarding

Tenants access a suite of residency tools including digital lease signing, a document vault, lease overview with renewal eligibility, a read-only unit map, unit transfer requests, and move-out requests. The platform includes interactive product tours and structured onboarding flows to guide new tenants through the Dashboard, Messages, Lease, and Community modules.

## Delimitations

The study is subject to the following constraints and exclusions, each of which reflects deliberate boundaries set to maintain research focus, operational feasibility, and legal compliance.

### A. Geographic and Demographic Boundaries

The system is limited to residential multi-unit properties — apartments, dormitories, and boarding houses — within Barangay Marulas, Canumay, Malinta, and Maysan in Valenzuela City. Single-family homes, transient hotels, and commercial properties are excluded, as these require distinct regulatory frameworks and management workflows not applicable to multi-unit residential tenancy. Expansion beyond Marulas, Canumay, Malinta, and Maysan is not attempted, as doing so would require coordination with multiple barangay councils, dilute the research focus, and prevent the team from establishing the local trust and physical verification capacity necessary for meaningful user testing. Tenant demographics are not segmented by age, income, or employment type, in observance of Data Privacy Act requirements and to avoid enabling discriminatory filtering within the platform.

### B. Property and Unit Modeling Constraints

The 2D Spatial Canvas is an organizational visualization tool only and does not produce engineering-grade measurements, structural load computations, building code compliance assessments, or fire safety evaluations. These activities constitute professional practice restricted to licensed engineers and architects under Philippine law. The floor planner uses a simplified rectangular grid and does not support irregular or curved geometry, as such features would introduce significant development complexity while offering minimal added value given that the vast majority of multi-unit residential properties in the target area use standard rectangular unit layouts. Three-dimensional rendering, photorealistic visualization, and BIM integration are likewise excluded. Unit status overlays are informational and do not automatically trigger maintenance dispatch or penalty enforcement, as automated actions of this nature require verified contractor networks and a liability framework the platform has not established. The system does not generate automated eviction notices, as eviction is a judicial process requiring attorney supervision under Batas Pambansa Blg. 25.

### C. Access Model and Tenant Onboarding Constraints

The platform operates exclusively as a private, landlord-managed system. There is no public-facing discovery portal, unit listing, property search, map-based filtering, or self-service tenant registration. All tenant accounts are provisioned by landlords or system administrators through approved onboarding and lease-finalization workflows. This design is intentional — tenant access to any property is gated by the landlord's explicit approval process, reflecting the real-world landlord–tenant onboarding dynamic in the target market. Consequently, advanced spatial analytics such as walkability scores, crime overlays, flood hazard maps, turn-by-turn navigation, and commute-time optimization are excluded from the system scope, as these features are relevant only to public discovery portals and external marketplace syndication.

### D. Messaging and Communication Limits

Messaging is limited to in-app text and media sharing (files and images within conversation threads) and does not include voice calls or video conferencing, which would require WebRTC infrastructure and significant bandwidth resources inconsistent with the target demographic's connectivity. The system uses standard HTTPS/TLS transport security and database encryption at rest but does not implement end-to-end encryption, as doing so would prevent admin moderation and create key recovery complications for legally significant communications such as lease discussions and payment confirmations. The platform does not integrate with external SMS gateways or email marketing systems, nor does it support user-programmable notification workflows. Automated notification rules are limited to predefined events such as payment reminders, maintenance updates, and lease expiration alerts, which cover the majority of operational communication needs.

### E. Lease and Document Handling

The enhanced digital lease signing workflow supports both in-person (dual) and remote (async) signing modes with tenant-first signing order enforcement, secure JWT-based signing links, and a comprehensive audit trail. However, the platform does not provide third-party notarization, as remote digital notarization is not legally recognized in the Philippines and requires physical appearance before a commissioned notary public under the 2004 Rules on Notarial Practice. The platform does not assess the legal enforceability of uploaded contracts, provide dynamically generated legal clause templates, manage dispute resolution or court filing processes, or perform automated redlining and version comparison of lease amendments. These activities either constitute the practice of law under Rule 138 of the Rules of Court or require legal expertise and ongoing monitoring of legislative changes that are beyond the scope of this study. Document templates are provided in basic form with clearly marked customization sections, with the expectation that landlords consult legal counsel for contract-specific provisions.

### F. Financial Operations and Payments

The system records payment schedules and invoice history but does not execute actual fund transfers or integrate with payment gateways, bank APIs, or GCash APIs. Operating as a payment intermediary would require BSP registration under Circular 649, AMLA compliance, KYC infrastructure, and PCI-DSS certification — obligations that are not feasible within the scope of an academic research project. Receipt verification is performed manually by landlords reviewing uploaded GCash screenshots, and no automated OCR-based fraud detection is included, as this would require training data and API access that are not publicly available. The ledger does not compute tax obligations, VAT filings, or government remittance schedules, and is not a substitute for formal accounting books maintained under BIR regulations. Currency support is limited to Philippine Peso.

### G. Maintenance and Work Orders

Maintenance requests are categorized through AI assistance but do not automatically assign contractors, as doing so would require a vetted vendor network and a formal liability framework. The platform does not include vendor bidding, procurement workflows, inventory management, or integration with external facilities management platforms. Work order scheduling is basic and does not optimize staff routing or labor allocation. Building inspection schedules, safety certification renewals, and compliance tracking remain the landlord's responsibility and are not managed within the system.

### H. AI Utility Boundaries

All AI-generated outputs within iReside are advisory and require human confirmation before any action is taken. The AI does not autonomously approve budgets, contracts, or legal decisions, and does not provide legal advice or guarantee the accuracy of its recommendations, as doing so would constitute unauthorized practice of law without the licensure required under Rule 138 of the Rules of Court. The AI knowledge base is configured by landlords from curated content and does not perform real-time web scraping, which would introduce unreliable sources, copyright complications, and unpredictable outputs. Credit scoring and automated tenant background checks are excluded due to the unavailability of credit bureau partnerships, the Data Privacy Act's requirements for human review of automated decisions affecting individuals, and the risk of algorithmic bias against informal workers and unbanked populations.

### I. Administrative Governance

Admin vetting validates document presence and performs visual inspection of uploaded permits and IDs but does not authenticate documents against government databases. The Valenzuela City Business Permit and Licensing Office (BPLO) and other relevant agencies do not provide public-facing APIs, and formal data-sharing agreements require institutional accreditation not available to this research project. Admin tools do not include full compliance auditing, periodic regulatory reporting, or management of landlord licensing renewals and tax registration. The admin role is limited to platform policy enforcement and does not extend to arbitrating legal disputes, which must be resolved through barangay conciliation or the appropriate courts under existing Philippine law.

### J. Security and Privacy Constraints

The system employs role-based access control with strong authentication but does not implement zero-trust networking, military-grade encryption, or enterprise SIEM integrations, as these exceed the security requirements and budget of a research-phase rental management platform. Data is hosted on cloud infrastructure; on-premise deployments are not supported. The platform requires an active internet connection and does not support offline-first synchronization, as conflict resolution for offline data modifications would introduce significant architectural complexity. Biometric authentication is excluded due to Data Privacy Act obligations for sensitive personal information, liveness detection requirements, and device compatibility constraints. Government-issued IDs submitted during landlord vetting are retained only as long as necessary for verification purposes, consistent with data minimization principles under RA 10173.

### K. Device, Platform, and Integration Limits

The platform is delivered as a unified web application built with Next.js 16 and React 19, providing both desktop-optimized and mobile-optimized Progressive Web App (PWA) interfaces through responsive design patterns. The mobile experience features touch-optimized UI components, screen-based navigation, and mobile-first CSS modules that adapt to smaller viewports while maintaining full feature access. The system supports all modern web browsers on desktop, Android, and iOS devices through standard web technologies. Native mobile applications for Android or iOS are not included in the current version, as developing and maintaining separate native codebases would exceed the capstone timeline and budget, and the PWA approach provides adequate cross-platform coverage for the target user base. The system does not provide open APIs for third-party developers, integrate with government housing or national ID verification databases, or connect with external listing marketplaces. Data export is limited to operational PDF reports and CSV files; large-scale data warehousing, ETL pipelines, and BI platform integrations are outside the scope of this study. Push notifications may be delivered through browser-based web push APIs where supported by the user's device and browser.

### L. Analytics, Reporting, and Content

Reporting within the system is operational and descriptive. Predictive analytics, forecasting, and dynamic pricing optimization are excluded, as these require multi-year historical datasets, external economic data integrations, and machine learning expertise beyond the research team's capacity. The system does not include a custom BI dashboard builder or blend external data sources into its analytics. Media is compressed and stored for web-optimized performance rather than archival quality. The system does not provide multilingual localization beyond English with basic Filipino terminology, as the target audience of Valenzuela City residents is served adequately by this combination.

### M. Community Hub Constraints

The Community Hub supports building-wide engagement through posts, polls, photo albums, and announcements within the private property ecosystem. However, posts are limited to pre-defined types (Discussion, Photo Album, Resident Poll, Management Notice, Utility Alert), and the platform does not support custom post formats, threaded sub-communities, or public-facing social media integration. Content moderation is handled through landlord approval queues and user-driven content reporting, but the platform does not implement automated AI-based community post moderation beyond the messaging layer.

### N. Legal, Ethical, and Operational Boundaries

The platform does not provide legal counsel on tenancy disputes or eviction procedures, generate government compliance reports, or make automated housing denial decisions. Any automated decision-making that could affect tenant rights must involve meaningful human review in compliance with Section 20 of the Data Privacy Act (RA 10173). The system does not collect biometric, medical, or financial credit data, consistent with data minimization principles and fair housing considerations. Terms of use and privacy policies included in this research version are generic and should be reviewed by legal counsel prior to any commercial deployment. The platform assumes that landlord-provided data is accurate and does not perform independent third-party verification of property details. Standard cloud hosting availability applies; the system does not guarantee high availability SLAs, and disaster recovery is limited to regular database backups. The current version does not support automated lease renewal or IoT integrations, and does not include advanced GIS layers, cadastral mapping, multi-city scaling, or an iOS version — all of which are identified as future work contingent on regulatory partnership, infrastructure investment, and post-research commercialization.
